# AI Trust
I usually do not trust the AI while coding, as it can easily lead to vibecoding and ruin my code (which is also a situation of low reliability). However, during feedbacking, since I don't have anyone to check my code, I have to partially trust the AI in this matter.

# What I Learned
I have learnt that software development is not too difficult as long as you are the main controller of your code, not the AI. I have also learnt to not depend too much on the AI tools, and to minimize its' usage a lot. The only time I should trust AI is when I have to ask for feedback when I have no one to do so, so that means to use AI when I do not have any choice.

# Reflection
AI somewhat improved the speed, as I dont have to ask and wait for my peers to respond to my request for checking. I also did feel most in control of my code. I would still use the AI the same way next time (limiting myself unless I REALLY need to).
